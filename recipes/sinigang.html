Sinigang


Description

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines.

The quality of this dish depends on the souring agent. The most common and widely used is unripe tamarind. It is known as sampaloc in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 1 bunch kang-kong
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 12 pcs string beans/sitaw
  • 2 pcs tomato
  • 3 pcs chili pepper
  • 1 tbps cooking oil
  • 1 onion sliced
  • 2 pcs gabi
  • 1 pack sinigang mix

Steps

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil
  2. Saut√© the onion until its layers separate from each other
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown
  4. Put-in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil
  6. Add the gabi and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  7. Put-in the sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add the string beans/sitaw (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes
  9. Put-in the kang-kong, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the kang-kong cook using the remaining heat in the pot.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
